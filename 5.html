About the Speaker

Theodora Scarato has long worked on children’s environmental health issues in the schools and was instrumental in the Prince George’s County School System move to address lead contamination in drinking water and in the Maryland State Advisory Council on Children's Environmental Health Protection move to issue recommendations to reduce radiofrequency electromagnetic (EMF) exposures in the school setting. She presented at the recent US medical conference on EMFs. Scarato has lectured at NIH and internationally on policy regarding children’s health and electromagnetic radiation. Her latest publication is on how to reduce electromagnetic exposures in buildings. More at Ehtrust.org
